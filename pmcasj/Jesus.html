Jesus is the Bread of Life

The Last Supper

Jesus answered and said to them, "This is the work of God, that you believe in the one he sent." (John 6:29-51)

So Jesus said to them, "Amen, amen, I say to you, it was not Moses who gave the bread from heaven; my Father gives you the true bread from heaven. (John 6:32)

For the bread of God is that which comes down from heaven and gives life to the world." (Jn. 6/33)

I am the living bread that came down from heaven; whoever eats this bread will live forever; and the bread that I will give is my flesh for the life of the world." (John 6:51)

Old Testament Promises and Prophecy

In the Old Testament God as Light is revealed through the mouths of the prophets by words and images. The lamp gives light; the path for ones feet is lit by God's light. Light is life; one is with the living when in the presence of those who keep God's word.

New Testament Covenant Fulfilled

In the New Testament the Word comes into the world in the person of Jesus. The image becomes Reality; the metaphor becomes the Master. The Word Incarnate, is the Light of the world. He is the Light of life. To be in his presence is to be in the light. The light of God's actions in Jesus contrasts with the darkness of men's actions. Men preferred the dark to the light --in this is judgement. In the Light we see light; we, ourselves, will know where our judgement lies. To be in God's Light is to be in Glory. That glory is meant to begin here on earth. To be in "glory" on earth means to be at peace with oneself --despite agitation, anger, or depression. God lives within the person.

"And the judgement is this: though the light has come into the world people have preferred darkness to the light because their deeds were evil" (John 3:19).

"I have come into the world as light, to prevent anyone who believes in me from staying in the dark any more" (John 12:46).

"...for you have saved my life from death to walk in the presence of God, in the light of the living" (Psalms 56:13).

"But if we live in light, as he is in light, we have a share in another's life, and the blood of Jesus, his Son, cleanses us from all sin" (1 John 1:7).

"...the unbelievers whose minds have been blinded by the god of this world, so that they cannot see shining the light of the gospel of the glory of Christ, who is the image of God" (2 Corinthians 4:4).

"When Jesus spoke to the people again, he said: I am the light of the world; anyone who follows me will not be walking in the dark, but will have the light of life" (John 8:12).
